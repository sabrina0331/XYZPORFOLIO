WASTEFREE MOBILE APP

Do you have those moments when you were standing in front of different recycling bins at a food court, feeling someone is watching you from behind, then you just throw your containers in the bin which you think is the right one?

Do those bins look familiar to you?

Photo by: Ashlea on Blogger

Why A Recycle App?

People feel challenged to recycle not only at a food court but also at home.

Due to a family visit to San Francisco, I spent a week in downtown SF. I observed that the building where I was staying near Union Square, even has two separate trash chutes(recycle and waste), but I still can see people throwing to-go boxes in the waste chute. As Global Warming continues year after year. I want to find out what challenges people to recycle.

How Might We help people to recycle better?

My Hypothese

Based on my experience, I came up with some hypotheses that why people don't recycle. What challenges do they face? What are the goals do I want to accomplish.

Challenges:

  • They don't want to waste time on sorting waste.
  • No one tells them whether they do it right or wrong.

Goals:

Educate people to recycle correctly.

Project Duration: One Month

Project Type:Conceptual, Solo Project

My Role: UX Designer, User Researcher

Design Process

I applied design thinking to this whole project. By completing one cycle of this process, now I have a deep understanding of this process and how to use this process to create Users' Centered design in real life.

I.EMPATHIZE

To find out the accuracy of my assumption, I conducted user interviews, and here are the findings.

With the above hypotheses in mind, I interviewed five people to better understand users' challenges. Interviewees aging 22-46 years old are working in different fields and order to-go meals every week. They are my target users. The findings are shocking.

Some interview questions Click to check out the whole interview

Key Findings

  • People who think recycling is easy but think they didn't do it right.
  • People recycle materials based on their experience.
  • People have a hard time telling if some materials are recyclable.

II.DEFINE

By conducting the user research, I found something different from my expectations that people actually want to recycle, they are overwhelmed by all the recycling information and don't what to do.

Goals After User Research:

  • Educate people to recycle correctly.
  • Find an easier way to help people to sort waste.
  • Encourage people to recycle better.

Are there any recycling-related apps on the market?

Most sustainability-related applications online right now such as iRecycle, Recycle Coach, Recycle! are focused on tracking recycling trucks and locating your nearby recycling bins. I dug into user's reviews from those competitor apps,and did a competitive analysis and found out the following:

Three main competitors

Key Findings

  • Users think a reminder of recycling pick-up is a great touch but sometimes the reminder is not accurate.
  • Users like the feature of "find nearby recycling center".
  • There should have a sorting guide that users can search a specific material.

III.IDEATE

I refined the features of the WasteFree app based on User research and competitor analysis.

Because WasteFree focuses on finding an easier way to recycle better, I listed the features below as core features of this app, and I used Crazy 8's to sketch out app wireframes.

  • Scan to sort waste: Save users time to figure out which material is recyclable without scanning through all sorting guide.
  • Save scanned results: Reinforce users to memorize the material next time.
  • Nearby Waste Disposable Center: Helps user to locate the recycling center and give directions on specific maps.
  • Recycling news: Helps users improve their sustainability and recycling rate.
  • Volunteering opportunity: Connects users with others to learn and recycle better in the future.

In order to create a easier way for users to recycle better by using this app, I created a User Flow with tasks that include: User gets sorting guide by scanning a material, User checks out articles (News), User registers a volunteering event, User finds a recycling center.

What I learned

What you think is not what the USERS think.

This is a very exciting application for me because I think recycling is a real issue we need to solve. This project helped me realize First, what a designer think is not what users think. Second, Sometimes what users say is not what they think. So when I was doing user interviews, I observed how users interact with the app with given tasks, and I asked how they feel when finishing each task. For the final design, I combined both results from those interviews to refine users centered application.

Next Step

Take a Data-Driven approach to UX Design

Since I create this project based on my personal experience and my target audiences are very specific(people would like to recycle better), but to help realistic global warming problem, I need to expand the range of my target audience. Next step, I'll conduct another usability test and expand the range of my interviewees to see if this idea can reach more people's needs.
